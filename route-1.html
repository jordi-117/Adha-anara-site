Birds of the Rural Mosaic in La Chaquira

Description

You will explore a region where ancient history coexists with birds native to the upper Magdalena River. Along the way, you will observe Andean species while enjoying the river landscapes and the agricultural areas that characterize the region. This experience highlights the importance of conservation and how it fosters the presence of birds in the area. Their colors, songs, and movements will accompany you throughout the journey, becoming the central element of the adventure.

Location

Hamlet La Chaquira, Municipality of San Agustín, Department of Huila, Colombia

Difficulty

Easy to Moderate

Altitude

1,700 – 2,000 m

Best time

Year round

Group size

Max 6 people

What to bring

Comfortable hiking shoes, warm jacket, hat, binoculars, camera

Climate

Cool to cold in altitude, occasional rain, warm days in valleys

Ecosystems

Cloud forests, moorlands, agricultural landscapes

Expert guide

Professional birding guide with extensive local knowledge

Tour Plan

01

Early morning birding along rural trails and forest edges. Observation of endemic and near-endemic species.

02

Observation of coffee plantations and mixed agricultural landscapes. Spotting colorful tanagers and hummingbirds.

03

Strategic stops for endemic and regional bird species. Exploration of cloud forest habitats and moorland ecosystems.

04

Return walk with panoramic views of the Magdalena Valley. Final observations and species documentation.

05

Optional evening birding session. Photography opportunities during golden hour with scenic mountain backdrops.

Key Bird Species

This route offers excellent opportunities to observe colorful tanagers, iridescent hummingbirds, shy antpittas, and incredible toucans. You may encounter endemic species found only in Colombian highlands, including specialized moorland birds and cloud forest specialists. Photography opportunities are excellent throughout the day.
